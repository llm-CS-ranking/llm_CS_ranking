% ABSTRACT
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\begin{abstract}
Can frontier LLMs be evaluated as factor-like cross-sectional rankers rather than as point forecasters?
We study 11 included LLMs from OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic on 30 NASDAQ-100 constituents using a controlled listwise ranking protocol, with expected return as the primary objective and Sharpe/Sortino prompts as robustness checks.
Each monthly ranking is converted into quintile portfolios and evaluated with rank IC, FF6 spanning regressions, within-universe style fingerprints, common-window checks, and transaction costs.
We find that (i) model choice materially changes the induced factor profile: in the conservative window, o3 reaches a long--short Sharpe of 5.54 and IC of 0.59, while GPT-4o variants produce weak or negative spreads;
(ii) the strongest LLM portfolios in this benchmark carry large FF6 alphas with positive momentum and negative value/profitability/investment loadings, consistent with a growth--momentum implicit prior;
and (iii) input ablations show that market summaries reproduce most of the full-input factor profile, whereas fundamentals-only prompts weaken both alpha and style coherence.
The evidence is a controlled model-comparison benchmark rather than a production trading claim: choosing an LLM changes the implicit factor exposure being tested.
\end{abstract}
